Saturday March 6. 1920 –

Dearest B.B –

So many thanks for your two letters which I found here on my arrival last night – an utterly exhausted human specimen. The passage was extraordinarily fine, but if I poked my head of out of my stateroom I was talked to death & the fog-horn, the last 14 hours almost drove me insane – The advice I have received of the intercepted R.R. service taken in conjunction with your letters, has decided me to proceed to London – I therefore plan to leave here on the 12th – but shall not be surprised to find at the last moment that there is no train [^boat^] service –

I shall probably have to get to Rome by April 15th at the latest and hope to be able to get accomodations [sic] at the Excelsior or Grand – Please thank
