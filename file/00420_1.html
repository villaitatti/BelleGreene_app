Monday Nov. 6

Dearest B.B. –

I have this morning received your letters of Oct. 28 & 31 – From my letter of yesterday you will see that I know only too well the difficulties of crossing and returning and evidently I know more of the reasons than you do – at least as evinced by your letters & that they will continue for some time – My Chief has absolutely put his

[p. 2]

foot down on my going back in any but an American boat & if you come over in August I strongly give you the same advice. It is not safe. You will receive my telegram before this telling you I have had to definitely give up the idea of getting to Paris

I never had any intention of joining with S– I thought it would amuse me to string him for a while – I am wiring him that I cannot get over
