May 2, 1920

Sunday –

I could not continue as I had to run for a dinner engagement – I have had the most delightful day – winding up with a "Gambol" with 3 American boys from our Embassy – they took me for dinner to a place called Castells di Cesare, which is probably very common, but where I had the best food I have eaten here and the most enchanting views from the

[p. 5]

balcony where we ate. Then we rode to the Colisseum [sic] and walked all around it. It was the most enchanting moonlight & I revelled in it as would the very most ordinary tourist – I am sure that I shall not forget one moment of my time there to my dying day. Nothing more remote from your circle at I Tatti, than these kid boys, could possibly be imagined – I was a bit upset when they insisted upon singing "over there" and "another little drink" inside the Colisseum [sic], but, nevertheless
