Tuesday Oct. 19 – 1915

[Note: letterhead crossed out with ink]

Dearest B.B. –

Your letter of Sept. 28th awoke me at last to a full realization of my "backsliding" – I really do not know how to explain to you the lethargy of melancholy in which I have been all the summer – I have gone through my work and am living during that time in a truly somnambulistic state – for the most part hating existence and in my

[p. 2]

happiest moments ignoring it – I really think it is all due to the war – it got into my system and into my nerves and it is a regular occurence [sic] for me to awake once at least during the night from some horrible nightmarish dream dream [sic] of it all – I could not endure the idea of seeing anybody or going anywhere and my few best friends have just about given me up – I have not written a (personal) line to anybody since I last wrote you
