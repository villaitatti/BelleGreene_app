Dec. 27. 1919.

Dearest B.B.

Thank you so much for your Christmas letter & your good wishes – Especially those for the 3rd and 4th generations –

About de Ricci –

I have been making a real study of him since he has been here – not because I am sure that he is worth it, but because I want to try to fathom that kind of a human being. My conclusion is that he is – so far in his career – quite honest, really childishly simple in many ways, inordinately greedy in every way – very "close" financially – on the make generally – and the most bungling and tactless creature on Earth – above all, devoted, heart and soul and with every pore in his body to [underlined twice: himself] first, last and always –

His insatiable curiosity in regard to everything belonging to everybody in or in any way bearing on every subject of which he is making a "list" (his specialty!) accounts for a good deal of his gaucherie.
