Sunday, Jan. 10, 1915

Dearest B.B. –

My real "heart" thanks for your letters which have literally kept me alive during the past ten days – Please accept that from me as a fact, no matter. how much it may astonish you. I have slept scarcely two hours a night during that time and all my waking hours have been so surcharged with excitement that at this minute I ought to be somewhere undergoing a rest cure. – all because that damned Fairfax Murray was paid to $50,000 to do something which he did not do – and which, as I prophesied when he first came over, I would eventually have to do – namely the disposing of these damned art objects.
