Monday, Oct. 23

Dearest B.B. –

As I telegraphed you this morning, I have only just received your two letters – They came together & as there is [double underlined: no] way of telling the date from those Italian scratches called postmarks – I – of course, read the later one first – They threw me into a dreadful quandary – although I realized immediately that London was hopeless – not only must I be

[p. 2]

constantly on the jump for business reasons but I dare not hope for much privacy – Everything is under watchful eyes, of necessity as I can well understand and to add to my troubles they told me this morning after I had telephoned you that the house was packed and crowded and that every room (mine included) had been taken months ahead for the winter. You see my plans were made (I must say for me) at such short notice
